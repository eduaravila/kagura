## X は Y + です

> we can say は start and gives the topic of a sentence and the next ones if the context is omited
> ** は (prounced wa) in this occasion**

## X + ですか？

Similar to the previous structure just adding the か at the end we can create questions

理由学生ですか？ are you an international student?
<iframe src="https://drive.google.com/file/d/1_DCs-2BSHZ_cfrrmH1HzxbekbbcjcVZN/preview" allow="autoplay"></iframe>

we can combine X は Y ですか？

メアリーさん　の　せんこう　は　なん　ですか。
meary san no senkoo wa nan desu ka?
which is the major of mary ? / what is mary studying ?

*is not obligatory to use a question mark at the end of a question sentence*

## Noun の, Noun

Noun1 の, Noun2
- the first noun could be treated as the "restriction"
- the second is the main idea of the phrase

**examples**
used to connect two nouns
桜大学の学生 -> student at university of sakura

健さん の お母さんは、高校の先生です
Ken's mother is a high school teacher.

in the above example
健さん の お母さん
works as a big noun giving us context
and the second part of the sentence tells us the main point

## 表現ノート (expression notes)
- あのー
	- when you have some observation but you don't want to interrupt the other person, or sound rude by interrupting
	- in other words lets say it's a way to say, i'm going to start talking o wanting to talk
- そうですか
	- "i see"
	- to let the other's know you have understood something

あなたの専攻はなんですか？

**木村さんは** who are we talking about

sakura university  no student
